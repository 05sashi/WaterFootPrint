WATER FOOTPRINTS

A water footprint is a measure of the total amount of water used directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, product, or country. It helps to assess the environmental impact of water use and can be a tool for managing water resources sustainably.

Types of Water Footprints:

  • Green Water Footprint: The volume of rainwater stored in the soil used by plants for growth.
  • Blue Water Footprint: The volume of freshwater withdrawn from surface or groundwater for activities like irrigation and domestic use.
  • Grey Water Footprint: The amount of freshwater required to dilute pollutants and contaminants from specific processes.
